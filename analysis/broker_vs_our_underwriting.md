# Broker Pro Forma vs. Our Underwriting

**Purpose:** Line-by-line reconciliation between the broker's stated NOI and our independent underwriting.

---

## The Headline Divergence

| | Broker Pro Forma | Our Underwriting |
|---|---|---|
| Effective Gross Income | $426,791 | $414,930 |
| Total Operating Expenses | $178,720 | $180,210 |
| **Net Operating Income** | **$248,070** | **$234,720** |
| **Cap Rate on Asking ($3.12M)** | **7.95%** | **7.52%** |
| **Cap Rate on T-12 basis** | — | **6.81%** |

The broker's 7.95% cap rate reflects normalized "Current" NOI, not actual T-12 performance. The **actual T-12 NOI is $212,574** — a 6.81% cap on asking price. This is the number we anchor to.

---

## Where the Numbers Diverge

### 1. Initial Rent Positioning

**Broker:** Assumes immediate rent lift from $904 current to $954 potential — a 5.5% initial jump justified by "6% below market" positioning.

**Us:** Independent rent-comp verification shows Windmill studios and 1BRs are already at or above per-sqft market. We do not underwrite meaningful initial rent lift on the 28 already-renovated units. Only the 5 remaining unrenovated units can be pushed to renovated rent (~$50/unit) after ~$7,000/unit capex.

**Divergence:** Broker adds ~$20K annualized rent; we don't.

### 2. Rent Growth Curve (Years 2-5)

**Broker:** Y1 2.1%, Y2 2.0%, Y3 2.1%, Y4 3.0%, Y5 3.0% — average ~2.4%.

**Us:** Flat 2.5% across all years.

**Divergence:** Nearly identical. Not a source of meaningful disagreement.

### 3. Expense Growth (Years 2-5)

**Broker:** Y1 1.3%, Y2 2.2%, Y3 2.1%, Y4 2.5%, Y5 3.0% — average ~2.2%.

**Us:** Flat 3.0% across all years to preserve margin discipline against inflation.

**Divergence:** We compress margins ~50-80bps per year vs. broker. This creates modest NOI pressure over the 5-year hold.

### 4. Vacancy & Collections

**Broker:** 5.0% physical vacancy + 1.0% collections loss = 6.0% total leakage.

**Us:** 5.0% physical vacancy + 3.5% collections loss = 8.5% total leakage.

**Rationale:** T-12 actual collections write-offs were 5.6%. Broker's 1% assumes issues immediately vanish under new ownership — unrealistic. Our 3.5% reflects meaningful but incomplete improvement.

**Divergence:** ~$8K annual EGI reduction vs. broker.

### 5. Repairs & Maintenance

**Broker:** $26,400 ($800/unit).

**Us:** $16,500 ($500/unit).

**T-12 actual:** $7,947 ($241/unit) — unsustainably low; seller front-loaded capex.

**Rationale:** For a 1980-vintage property with ongoing turnover, $500/unit is normalized run-rate. Broker's $800/unit is defensible but conservative; we go moderate.

**Divergence:** We save ~$10K vs. broker but spend ~$9K more than T-12.

### 6. Marketing & Advertising

**Broker:** $5,775 ($175/unit).

**Us:** $8,250 ($250/unit).

**T-12 actual:** $11,951 ($362/unit).

**Rationale:** Broker halves the T-12 line without explanation. We split the difference at $250/unit, which is a defensible run-rate for a Class C 33-unit property.

### 7. G&A

**Broker:** $8,250 (rounded down from OM p.24 stated $10,064).

**Us:** $10,064 (matches OM stated T-12).

**Divergence:** ~$2K annually.

### 8. Payroll

**Broker:** $0 (eliminated via third-party PM assumption).

**Us:** $0 (same assumption, adopted).

**T-12 actual:** $43,900 in-place payroll.

**Adopted rationale:** Third-party PM is a real and common structure for 33-unit deals. Broker's assumption is defensible. We flag that if the PM contract requires a separately-billed maintenance tech, $15-25K of the $43,900 savings could evaporate.

### 9. Management Fee

**Broker:** 8.0% of EGI = ~$34,143.

**Us:** 8.0% of EGI = ~$33,194 (varies slightly because our EGI is lower).

**Divergence:** Nominal.

## The Meta-Point

Every broker-produced pro forma normalizes T-12 in ways that flatter the cap rate. This is not deceptive — it's standard practice. The buyer's job is to identify which normalizations are defensible (like third-party PM structure) and which are aggressive (like 1% collections loss on a property with 5.6% write-offs).

**Our underwriting produces a lower NOI than the broker's, but a higher NOI than the T-12 actual.** This is exactly the "trust but verify" middle ground a serious buyer would land on.

The recommendation to bid at $2.75M rather than ask is directly driven by this analysis: at the honest NOI of ~$235K (before reserves), asking price yields a 7.5% cap — reasonable for a stabilized asset, insufficient for a value-add deal.
